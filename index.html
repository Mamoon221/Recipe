Brief history:

Mutton shinwari is a curry of Pashtun cuisine originating from the Shinwari tribes in Peshawar. This authentic Pashtun recipe involves cooking the mutton in the traditional method of cooking it in its own fat to give the curry a distinctive flavour and aroma. The use of no spices distinguishes it from other curries.

Mutton Karahi

Ingredients

  • 2 KG Mutton
  • Half Kg oil
  • 1/2 Kg Tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh green chilies, sliced, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a large bowl, combine the mutton pieces with yogurt, ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt.
  3. Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight for best results.
  4. In a separate skillet or pan, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes become soft and the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Transfer the marinated mutton to the pan with the masala.
  7. Lower the heat and cover the pan. Let the mutton cook on low heat for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the meat is tender and fully cooked.
  8. Garnish the Mutton Karahi with fresh coriander leaves, julienned ginger, and sliced green chilies.
  9. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
  10. Don't forget to serve lemon wedges on the side for extra tanginess.

    11. Enjoy your homemade Mutton Karahi with its rich, spicy flavors!
